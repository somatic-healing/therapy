About Jo

I'm Jo Chan, a Certified Internal Family Systems Practitioner and Somatic Healing Arts Practitioner with over twenty years of experience in mindfulness, yoga, Buddhist psychology, and trauma-informed therapy. I believe healing happens when all parts of you feel safe, seen, and valued. My intention is to offer gentle yet full presence—creating a compassionate, non-judgmental space where you can explore at your own pace, without rushing or fixing.

I specialize in combining Somatic IFS® and Somatic Experiencing® with the Internal Family Systems model. This means we work not just with your thoughts and emotions, but with the wisdom held in your body. Every pattern of behavior, thought, and feeling carries important meaning. When we listen deeply to what your body knows and welcome all parts of your experience, profound transformation becomes possible.

My approach is grounded in thousands of hours of personal retreat practice, extensive professional training, and my own healing journey through years of personal therapy. This allows me to accompany you with both clinical expertise and embodied understanding. Whether you have an existing meditation or mindfulness practice, or are new to somatic work, I'll meet you exactly where you are.

What to Expect

Our initial 90-minute consultation is a gentle introduction to the work. We'll explore what brings you here, begin to get acquainted with some of your parts, and see if we're a good fit. Every session is tailored to where you are and what you need.

Between Sessions

Healing unfolds in the spaces between our meetings too. I may invite you to explore gentle practices—brief check-ins with your parts, body awareness exercises, journaling, or mindfulness techniques—that support your ongoing process. These are always optional invitations, never homework.

Internal Family Systems (IFS)

What is IFS?

Internal Family Systems is an evidence-based psychotherapy that recognizes we all have different "parts"—sub-personalities with their own perspectives, feelings, and roles. Rather than pathologizing these parts, IFS honors that each one developed to help us survive and cope.

How It Works

Through gentle curiosity and compassion, we help you get to know your parts—the inner critic, the people-pleaser, the protector, the wounded child. As you build relationship with these parts and understand their positive intentions, they naturally begin to soften and transform.

The Role of Self

Beneath all the parts is your core Self—the calm, curious, compassionate presence that can lead your internal system. IFS helps you access this Self-energy and bring healing to the parts that need it most.

My Training

I'm a Level 3 Certified IFS Practitioner through the IFS Institute, with additional training in Somatic IFS® and IFIO (Intimacy from the Inside Out for relational work). I also serve as a Program Assistant for IFS trainings.

What to Expect

Sessions are gentle and collaborative. We move at your pace, never forcing. You might close your eyes and turn inward, or we might dialogue about what you're noticing. The work is about curiosity, not fixing—trusting that your system knows how to heal when given the right conditions.

Somatic Healing

Why the Body Matters

Trauma, stress, and emotional patterns aren't just stored in our minds—they live in our bodies. Your nervous system holds memories, tensions, and protective responses that talk therapy alone may not reach. Somatic work brings the body into the healing process.

Somatic IFS®

This approach combines traditional IFS with body-centered awareness. We track sensations, movements, and impulses as we work with your parts. A part might show up as tension in your chest, a heaviness in your shoulders, or an urge to curl up small. By including these somatic experiences, healing becomes more embodied and complete.

Somatic Experiencing®

Developed by Peter Levine, SE® helps resolve trauma by gently working with the body's natural capacity to discharge stuck survival energy. We pay attention to subtle shifts in sensation, breath, and nervous system activation—supporting your body to complete defensive responses that may have been interrupted during overwhelming experiences.

What Happens in Session

I might invite you to notice sensations in your body, track your breath, or explore gentle movements. We work slowly and titrate—never overwhelming your system. The goal is to help your nervous system feel safe enough to release, reorganize, and restore its natural resilience.

My Training

I'm trained in Somatic IFS® (including serving as Staff Assistant to Susan McConnell), Somatic Experiencing® at the Advanced Level, Trauma-Sensitive Yoga, Hakomi somatic psychotherapy, and Thai-Vedic Bodywork. I bring 20+ years of embodied practice through yoga, meditation, and mindful movement.

Contemplative Integration

Bridging Inner & Outer Work

If you have an existing meditation, mindfulness, or spiritual practice, we can weave it into our therapeutic work. Many practitioners find that combining contemplative practice with psychotherapy creates powerful synergy—each deepening the other.

My Background

I bring over 20 years of dedicated practice in mindfulness meditation, Buddhist psychology, yoga, and thousands of hours of of retreat practices. This isn't just theory—it's lived experience that informs how I show up in session and the practices I might share with you.

What This Looks Like

We might explore how your meditation practice intersects with your parts work. Perhaps a protective part resists sitting still, or a spiritual bypassing part uses practice to avoid difficult feelings. We can work with these dynamics compassionately, helping your practice become more integrated and authentic.

Practices I Might Share

Depending on what serves you, I might offer guidance in mindfulness techniques, body scan practices, loving-kindness meditation, breathwork, or somatic awareness exercises. These are always invitations, never prescriptions—tailored to your needs and comfort level.

Not Required
